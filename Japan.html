Japan; 日本

Tokyo Tower

Did you know that the Tokyo tower is actually taller than the Eiffel tower? Did you also know that the tower gets repainted every 5 years? And apparently, they change its color on special occassions! On Oct 1st, the tower becomes pink to promote breast cancer awareness. On Nov 14th, the tower becomes blue to increase awareness for diabetes. The two main purposes of this tower is for broadcasting and tourism. Approximately 3 million people come to visit the tower annually. There is an aquarium in the 1st floor of the tower!! On a clear day, you can see Mt. Fuji.

Meiji Shrine

Did you know that the Meiji shrine was burned down during World War II? It was later rebuilt thanks to public donations. Here you can purify your hands and mouth from the communal water tank, and write wishes on little pieces of paper and tie them on the prayer wall. You can toss some money in the offering box as well. Bow your head twice, clap twice and bow once again. If you want to see a traditional Japanese wedding, go on a Sunday. At the opposite end of the shrine, there is an inner garden, which looks espicialy stunning in the summer.

Shibuya

Shibuya crossing is known as the busiest intersection in the world. When the lights change, hundreds of people from every direction cross at once. Especially on a Friday night, this place is packed with people. Yet everyone manages to successfully go to their respective destinations. Shibuya has fashion stores, nightclubs, dining places, record shops and many more. The famous Hachiko statue is located right outside the Shibuya station. This statue was made for a dog who came to meet his owner after work at this station every day. After the owner died, the dog still continued to wait for his owner.

Asakusa

Asakusa is an increasingly trendy place. They have temples and they host many events. They have fireworks during the summer, they have a parade of dancers and musicians and so on. There is the Nakamise Dori, which is a long shopping street that leads up to the temple. They have stores along the way, where they sell Japanese dolls, kimonos, kitchen silverware, samurai stuff, snacks, and so on. It's a great place to buy Japanese souvenirs and to try out various Japanese street food.
